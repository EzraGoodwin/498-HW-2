========== Analysis/Discussion ==========
Instance A had significantly lower latency of 67ms for
/register and 61.10ms for /list compared to Instance B's latency of
287.30ms and 235.60ms respectively. This is expected because I am
located in the US, so requests to the us-central1 instance travel
a much shorter physical distance than requests to europe-west1.
Additionally, since the Firestore database is hosted in us-central1,
Instance B must also cross the Atlantic to read and write data which adds further delay.

My eventual consistency experiment showed 0 out of 100 cases where
a username registered on Instance A was not immediately visible on
Instance B. This suggests that in my experiment, Firestore's replication
lag is smaller than the network round-trip time of our sequential
requests. By the time my /list query reached Instance B, the data
had already propagated. This could be because of the slow squential nature of my tests.
